Victorian Motor Accidents

Comparison Between Metropolitan and Country

Number of Accidents

There was a total of 74,908 motor accidents in Victoria from July 2013 to March 2019. Metropolitan Victoria had a total of 53,257 motor accidents Country Victoria had a total of 21,651 motor accidents.

Across the years 2014 to 2018 there was annual patterns in accident numbers during the different months. There are significant increases during March and October for the number of motor accidents. Whereas accident numbers decreased in June and September.

The number of accidents of the years have shown a decreasing trend with 2018 having a much lower number of accidents than 2014.

Motor Accidents per 1,000 People



On average, metropolitan Victoria had an average of 11.09 accidents per 1000 people whereas country Victoria was larger at an average of 15.49 accidents per 1000 people. Hence, Local Government Areas (LGA's) in country Victoria often more accidents per 1000 people than metropolitan Victoria.

Metropolitan

Number of Accidents by Day and Time

Crashes are most frequent during peak hour times which are Monday to Friday in the morning between 7-10am and in the afternoon between 3-6pm. Furthermore, on Friday night there are more accidents than other weekends. During the working hours crashes significantly decrease frequency Moreover, weekends often have significantly less crashes than the weekdays which is vastly different from country Victoria. However, there are still crashes around midday on Saturday and Sunday and late afternoon on Sunday.

Accidents by Type

The majority of accidents are due to a collision within two vehicles and the remaining mostly comprises of collisions with fixed objects or impact with a pedestrian. These three types of accidents make up over 90% of accidents in metropolitan Victoria. Furthermore, there is a considerably large number of accidents from drivers between the age of 18-25. Due to the difference in driving conditions in metropolitan Victoria compared to Country Victoria, the distribution of accidents varies significantly.

Accident Severity by Speed

The majority of accidents occur in the 60 and 100km/hr speed zones. Similar to metropolitan Victoria, as speed increases the proportion of serious injuries and fatal accidents increases. Although there is a similar trend of accident severity and speed zones between metropolitan and country Victoria, country Victoria often has a larger proportion of accidents resulting in a serious injury, particularly in higher speeds zones.

Country

Number of Accidents by Day and Time

Crashes are most frequent during weekends rather than weekdays. This may is likely to individuals travelling in long road trips or going to and from a country house over the weekend. Unlike metropolitan Victoria, early mornings have less crashes, however, there are more crashes during the work hours during weekdays. This is likely linked to more travel occurring in country regions during the day compared to metropolitan Victoria. Overall, the frequency of crashes is significantly different in country Victoria to metropolitan Victoria.

Accidents by Type

Almost half of the accidents are from collision with vehicles with a quarter being collision with fixed objects. Categories such as vehicle overturned and no collision are much more prominent in country Victoria likely due to the higher speed limits. However, collision with a vehicle and impact with pedestrian is much lower than metropolitan Victoria. The age composition among the accident types is very similar to metropolitan Victoria with a considerable amount arising from driver aged 18-25.

Accident Severity by Speed

Most accidents in metropolitan Victoria occur in the speed zones of 50, 60 and 80km/hr. For speed zones 30-60km/hr there are a lower proportion of accidents that were serious injuries and no fatal accidents. As speed increases there is a larger proportion of serious injuries and fatal accidents begin to occur more frequently. Thus, it is evident as speed increases so does the accident severity.
