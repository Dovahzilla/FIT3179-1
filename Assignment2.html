Victorian Motor Accidents

Comparison Between Metropolitan and Country

Number of Accidents

There was a total of 74,908 motor accidents in Victoria from July 2013 to March 2019. Metropolitan Victoria had a total of 53,257 motor accidents Country Victoria had a total of 21,651 motor accidents.

Among the years 2014 to 2018 there were patterns in accident numbers accross the different months. There were significantly more motor accidents during March and October. Whereas, accident numbers were lower in June and September.

The number of accidents over the years have shown a decreasing trend with 2018 having a much lower number of accidents than 2014.

Metropolitan

Number of Accidents by Day and Time

Accidents were most frequent during peak hour times. This was between 7-10am in the morning and between 3-6pm in the afternoon. Furthermore, on Friday night there were more accidents than the other weekdays. During the working hours, accidents were significantly lower. Moreover, weekends had considerably less accidents than the weekdays which is vastly different from country Victoria. However, accidents are still prominent during midday on Saturday and Sunday and in the afternoon on Sunday.

Accidents by Type

The majority of accidents were due to a collision between two vehicles and the remaining accidents comprised mostly from collisions with fixed objects and impacts with pedestrians. These three types of accidents resulted in over 90% of the motor accidents in metropolitan Victoria. Furthermore, there is a large number of accidents arising from drivers between the age of 18-25. The distribution of accident types varied significantly between metropolitan Victoria and Country Victoria due to differences in driving conditions.

Accident Severity by Speed Zones

Most accidents in metropolitan Victoria occured in the speed zones of 50, 60 and 80km/hr. For speed zones 30-60km/hr there are a lower proportion of accidents that were serious injuries and no fatal accidents. As speed increases there is a larger proportion of serious injuries and fatal accidents begin to occur more frequently. Thus, it is evident as speed increases so does the accident severity.

Country

Number of Accidents by Day and Time

Accidents were most frequent during weekends likely due to individuals travelling longer trips or going to and from a country house. Unlike metropolitan Victoria, early mornings had less accidents, however, there were more accidents in the work hours during weekdays. This is likely linked to more travel occurring in country regions during the day compared to metropolitan Victoria. Overall, the frequency of accidents was significantly different in country Victoria and metropolitan Victoria.

Accidents by Type

Almost half the accidents arose from a collision between vehicles with another quarter arising from collisions with fixed objects. Accidents categorised as ‘vehicle overturned’ and ‘no collision’ are much more prominent in country Victoria. However, collision with vehicles and impacts with pedestrians was much lower in country Victoria than metropolitan Victoria. The age composition among accident types is similar to metropolitan Melbourne with a considerable amount arising from drivers aged 18-25.

Accident Severity by Speed Zones

The majority of accidents occured in the 60 and 100km/hr speed zones. Similar to metropolitan Victoria, as speed increased the proportion of serious injuries and fatal accidents increased. There is a similar trend of accident severity and speed zones between metropolitan and country Victoria. However, country Victoria had a larger proportion of accidents resulting in a serious injury, particularly in higher speeds zones.
